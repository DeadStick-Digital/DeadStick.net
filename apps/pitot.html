Pitot

A self-contained document scanning and storage app for people who want their records searchable, organized, and private. Scan pages, extract text on device, keep documents sorted by folders and tags, and export clean PDFs without sending your files through a proprietary server.

Contact support → Learn more

Overview

Pitot is a local-first document scanner built around a private document library. It is designed for the small records people actually need to keep: receipts, notes, IDs, forms, paper files, and multi-page scans that should be easy to find again later.

The app starts with a document OS: recent scans, folders, tags, search, and quick camera capture. Behind that surface, Pitot keeps source images, OCR text, generated PDFs, annotations, and indexes inside the device-first library.

What you can do with it

  • Start a smart scan from the home screen and capture single or multi-page documents.
  • Use crop detection, perspective correction, and image cleanup to produce cleaner pages.
  • Extract text locally for searchable documents and page-level indexes.
  • Organize scans with folders, tags, recent documents, and quick search.
  • Export documents as PDFs and use local PDF tools for page-level operations.

How your data is stored

Pitot is designed to work without a DeadStick-operated backend. Documents, page images, OCR results, and generated PDFs stay in the local document library by default. Private sync through the user's own iCloud or Google Drive storage is planned as a provider-specific boundary rather than a proprietary Pitot server.

Platform

Coming soon: iOS 18 or later.
Planned: Android, with native Kotlin and Google Drive private app-data storage support.

Release channel

Pitot is in active development. The first public release will focus on local scanning, storage, search, OCR, and PDF export before private sync and advanced document tools roll out in later updates.
