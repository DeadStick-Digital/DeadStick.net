Holos Document Vault

A self-contained document scanning and storage app for people who want important records searchable, organized, translated, and ready for long-term reference. Scan pages, extract text on device, translate document text, keep documents sorted by folders and tags, and export clean PDFs without sending your files through a proprietary server.

Overview

Holos Document Vault is a local-first document scanner built around a private document vault. It is designed for the important records people need to keep long term: passports, driver's licenses, IDs, forms, and paper files that should be easy to find again later.

The app starts with a document vault: recent scans, folders, tags, search, and quick camera capture. Behind that surface, Holos Document Vault keeps source images, OCR text, generated PDFs, translations, annotations, and indexes inside the device-first vault.

What you can do with it

  • Start a smart scan from the home screen and capture single or multi-page documents.
  • Use crop detection, perspective correction, and image cleanup to produce cleaner pages.
  • Extract text locally for searchable documents and page-level indexes.
  • Keep passports, driver's licenses, IDs, forms, and other important records together.
  • Translate extracted document text while keeping the original scan in place.
  • Organize scans with folders, tags, recent documents, and quick search.
  • Use planned private sync to access your document vault through your own iCloud or Google Drive account across supported devices.
  • Export documents as PDFs and use local PDF tools for page-level operations.

The Premium Standard for Document Security

Standard scanning apps treat your documents as a product, monetizing them through third-party ad networks, excessive location tracking, and centralized cloud hosting that exposes you to data breaches. Holos Document Vault is engineered differently:

  • Zero Ad-Network Supply Chains: Free scanner apps monetize your attention by embedding third-party advertising frameworks, creating dangerous vulnerabilities for malware and device hijacking. Holos Document Vault is a premium tool with zero embedded ad networks. Your app remains clean, fast, and mathematically immune to third-party ad SDK exploits.
  • Enterprise-Ready Data Isolation: Sending documents to the cloud for OCR and translation violates strict corporate, government, and healthcare data-residency policies. Holos Document Vault performs all OCR text extraction, indexing, and translation natively on-device. No document data is ever sent to a remote indexing service, making it structurally compliant for high-security workflows.
  • Bring Your Own Cloud (BYOC): Storing sensitive tax forms, IDs, and medical records on proprietary company servers exposes you to massive centralized data breaches. We solve this structurally: Holos Document Vault operates strictly local-first and syncs only through your existing iCloud or Google Drive. We never host your documents, meaning we have zero access to your data—and our architecture is designed to keep anyone else from having access to it, either.
  • Strict Permission Scoping: We ask for the camera to scan, and nothing else. No background location tracking, no device fingerprinting, and no hidden analytics harvesting. Your vault is secured entirely by your device's native Biometrics or a dedicated encrypted PIN.

How your data is stored

Holos Document Vault is designed to work without a DeadStick-operated backend. Documents, page images, OCR results, translations, and generated PDFs stay in the local document vault by default. Private sync through the user's own iCloud or Google Drive storage is planned as a provider-specific boundary rather than a proprietary Holos Document Vault server.

Security measures

Important documents deserve a calm, predictable data path. Holos Document Vault is built around practical safeguards that reduce where your documents need to travel and who can see them.

  • Documents stay local by default. Scans, page images, OCR text, translations, search indexes, and generated PDFs live in the local vault unless you choose an export or future private sync path.
  • No proprietary Holos Document Vault document server. Local scanning and storage do not require a DeadStick-operated backend that receives or recovers your documents.
  • On-device OCR and search indexes. Searchable text is created and stored with the device-first document record, keeping lookup fast without making a remote indexing service part of the workflow.
  • File-backed assets use SHA-256 content identity. Large page assets are kept outside metadata storage and addressed by content hash so the vault can track page files deterministically.
  • Path and symlink escape checks. The local asset store rejects unsafe paths that try to leave the vault root before loading, saving, or deleting files.
  • Optional app unlock controls. Holos Document Vault is designed to support Face ID, biometrics, or a Holos Document Vault PIN stored in the device keychain for people who want an extra unlock step before opening the vault.
